ESCAPE ROOMS

Fun for all ages in this themed adventure complex

The Escape Room complex is comprised of five different venues, each of depicting a singular and specific educational aspect of Habait. The variety of experiences caters for a great range of visitors from toddlers to senior citizens, in several languages and game difficulty levels.


The Legends of King Solomon

Find clues and crack the secrets leading to operating the mechanical throne. Break codes in the animal language, aid the king and the bee identify the only real, non-artificial flower and discover the sheaves associated with the legendary siblings. 

First Temple Jerusalem

A long drought or sabotages by sieging enemy have caused the water sources to dry out. Collect clues, local dwellers testimonials and other evidences in the city of David to resolve the distress. While you unravel this mystery, become familiar with the structure and topography of Jerusalem, its infrastructures and three pools, as well as typical local characters roaming the streets. 

The Phoenicians and Judea

You are merchant explorers from Judea, joining the merchant Phoenician fleet on its way to north Africa. Use your wit in restoring the missing construction manual, and repair the ship that ran aground in front of the island. In the process, learn about the Phoenicians, their empire, relationships with Judea and Israel, and their contribution to the building of Jerusalem and the temple. 



The Mighty Five
Unlock the mysteries of these cultures through unique items and artefacts, locks, puzzle boxes and riddles. Understand their differences as well as similarities in religion, systems of government, customs, traditions and relationships between themselves and Judea.

Prove Zerubbabel's dynastic claims to leadership through Tel Dan and Mesha steles. Interview Ezra and Nehemiah and solve puzzles to open the crates including the capital and treasures donated by the Jews of Babylon and King Cyrus.

King David's Dynasty
You are the helpers of Prince Zerubbabel, the only surviving offspring of King David's dynasty. Obtain the proofs and evidence sneeded to allow Zerubbabel inherit Cyrus the Great's appointed president, Sheshbazzar, and construct the second temple. Prove Zerubbabel's dynastic claims to leadership through Tel Dan and Mesha steles. Interview Ezra and Nehemiah and solve puzzles to open the crates including the capital and treasures donated by the Jews of Babylon and King Cyrus.
